Every March I write a short note for anyone who asked me last year about the North End. Here is the 2026 version.

The short version: inventory is still thin — measurably thinner than in 2023 — but the buyer pool has become more discerning, and homes that need work are sitting longer than they did eighteen months ago. For well-prepared listings in the core of the neighborhood (roughly, between State and Hill, between 8th and the Boulevard), we are still seeing multiple offers within the first ten days.

What has changed, quietly, is the edge of the neighborhood. The blocks north of Fort Street, east of 13th, and immediately west of the Grove are all behaving a little differently than they were a year ago — the premium for being "technically in the North End" has compressed. If you're a buyer, that's an opportunity. If you're a seller in those pockets, it is worth a conversation about pricing strategy before the listing goes up.

The other change worth flagging is the new wave of historic-district accessory-dwelling applications being approved. There are now at least twenty homes in the neighborhood with permitted carriage-house ADUs in active rental use, and several more under construction. If you've ever wondered whether you can convert your detached garage, you probably can — carefully, and with an architect who has done it before.

As always: if you'd like specifics for a particular block or a particular house, we can talk.
