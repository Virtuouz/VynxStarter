## The quiet alternative to Sun Valley

Ninety minutes north of Boise, where Highway 55 bends through Garden Valley, this 5-acre parcel has 270 feet of frontage on the Middle Fork of the Payette. The cabin was built in 2016 by a husband-and-wife team from Bend, Oregon, using a simple cross-gable plan and very few materials: board-and-batten cedar, black corrugated roof, white plaster inside.

## Grid-optional

Full off-grid capability if desired: 12kW photovoltaic array, 30kWh battery, a 3,000-gallon cistern with UV filtration, and a backup propane generator. All systems are also connected to grid and municipal water for day-to-day use. Starlink installed and a separate metal-building workshop with its own EV charger.

## The river

Wade-fishable for bull trout and rainbow. A flat, gravelly put-in for kayaks and pack rafts is a short path down from the cabin. A soaking tub fed by the cistern sits on a cedar platform above the river bend — unheated, but the kind of thing you'll talk about later.
