## New, but not generic

Most of what's being built in South Meridian right now looks the same. This one doesn't. A small, design-forward builder laid out the home around a central kitchen-and-hearth room, with deliberately narrow corridors, nine-foot ceilings, and a palette of limewashed plaster, rift-sawn oak, and blackened steel.

## What stands out

The mudroom. It's real — built for boots, jackets, and dog towels, not a vanity bench. A secondary back-kitchen hides the second dishwasher, the microwave, and a full pantry with a window. Two of the four bedrooms share a Jack-and-Jill bath that is *actually* configured for two kids.

## Outside

A covered rear loggia extends the main living area by 340 square feet, with a ceiling-mounted heater and roll-down shades for the August afternoons. Established 8-foot hornbeams screen the side yard. Drip irrigation zoned separately from the turf zones.

## The neighborhood

Silverleaf is a small, conservation-oriented subdivision of fewer than 40 homes, with a community orchard and a ten-minute walk to the new Chinden Marketplace.
