## A century of quiet confidence

Set back behind a white-picket ivy hedge on the most walkable stretch of Harrison Boulevard, this Colonial Revival has been in just two families since its 1921 construction. The current owners — an architect and a rare-book curator — spent seven years returning the home to its original proportions while quietly upgrading every system behind the walls.

Original quarter-sawn oak floors run throughout the main level. The formal sitting and dining rooms retain their crown mouldings, transom windows, and a pair of restored Batchelder-tile fireplaces. The kitchen is new but deliberately un-new: honed soapstone counters, unlacquered brass, a La Cornue range tucked into the original chimney breast.

## Outside

The rear garden was designed by a Portland landscape architect around a century-old silver maple. A bluestone terrace steps down to a sunken lawn, a cutting garden, and a cedar garden house currently used as a writing studio. The detached carriage house has been rewired and insulated — it reads equally well as a two-car garage or a future accessory dwelling.

## Systems & details

Dual-zone HVAC (2022), copper supply lines, foam-insulated attic, Marvin clad-wood windows, EV-ready 200-amp service. Roof replaced in 2023 with standing-seam steel in a heritage-appropriate charcoal. A rare North End home that has been loved rather than flipped.
