## Architecture, on its own terms

Set into a southwest-facing hillside above Warm Springs, the house steps down the slope in three cantilevered volumes of board-formed concrete and charred-cedar shiplap. A cold-rolled steel canopy slices through the site, pulling you from the motor court into a double-height living space that opens to the valley on three sides.

The design is the work of a Sun Valley architect with clear nods to Olson Kundig — exposed structure, operable window walls on counterweighted cables, and finishes chosen for how they patina rather than how they stay new.

## Living spaces

Five bedrooms occupy the two upper volumes, including a primary suite with a soaking tub placed directly into a steel-framed bay. The lower volume is devoted to a bunk room, a 14-seat screening room, a gear mudroom with ski-boot drying, and direct walk-out to the lower yard and hot tub.

## Site

An aspen grove was preserved and extended by local landscape architects. A dry creek bed collects snowmelt and channels it through the native meadow. The driveway heat-trace, generator, and mechanicals were specified for long absences between visits.
